5 books for ages 0-5

Super Magic Happy Forest

It’s Tolkien for toddlers! When the Mystical Crystals of Life are stolen, five brave heroes go on an epic quest to get them back and save the Super Magic Happy Forest from the root of all evil. There's so much to see and talk about on every page of this hilarious story! Bursting with jokes and details to pore over, Super Happy Magic Forest is sure to be a hit with children and grown-ups alike!

What a Wonderful World

A stunningly beautiful book featuring the lyrics from What a Wonderful World, accompanied by joyful illustrations from award-winning illustrator Tim Hopgood. It follows one little boy as he discovers just how wonderful this world of ours can be. The book comes with an accompanying CD which includes the much-loved recording of the song by Louis Armstrong and an exclusive reading by Sophie Aldred.

This book just ate my dog!

Wanted: nice reader to show this naughty book who's boss!! In this wonderfully inventive book, Bella is taking her dog for a stroll across the page when something odd happens. Her dog disappears and it becomes apparent to Bella, her friend Ben, and the rescue services that peril lurks in the pages of this book. But where the police and fire brigade fail, you - the reader - can help!

Winnie’s Haunted House

Winnie and Wilbur are settling down for a lazy afternoon. But when Wilbur accidently smashes one of Winnie's vases he hides to evade suspicion . . . He then goes on to bring down Winnie's curtains and her chandelier - each time hiding himself away. Winnie begins to think her house must be haunted. She instigates what she thinks is a spell to fix a haunted house but ends up instead filling her house with skeletons, ghosts, spiders, bats and owls, unleashing a spooky side to Winnie’s house that readers have never seen before!

You Make Me Smile

It's the first snowfall of winter and the little girl in this story is filled with that sense of wonder we all experience when the world turns white. She builds a snowman and gives him a twig smile so that they may share a moment of happiness and know the promise of a friendship that will be renewed each year.This book captures the magic of a moment before it melts away. Now you can share in the happiness it brings, time after time.

Nixie: Wonky Winter Wonderland

Snow days are for sledging, skating and snowball battles and no-one is safe from Nixie the bad, bad fairy's expertly aimed snowballs or her mischievous wand. But one fairy is missing out on all of the fun, where is Fidget the butterfly fairy? It's up to Nixie to find her and prove that she's not such a bad, bad fairy but a rather ingenious one!

Olga da Polga

One little guinea-pig and a whole lot of mischief! From the very beginning there was not the slightest doubt that Olga da Polga was the sort of guinea-pig who would go places. Michael Bond's guinea-pig has been charming readers for more than 40 years. With wonderful colour illustrations by Catherine Rayner and a dazzling design, this is a gift edition for young readers to treasure.

The Wind in the Willows

Kenneth Grahame's classic story of adventure and loyalty has been stunningly re-imagined through the illustrations of David Roberts in this lovely gift edition. The Wind in the Willows is a story full of beautifully-drawn characters. It is cherished from one generation to the next and so makes a perfect choice for a gift book as it will be enjoyed in equal measure by those tucked up in bed to hear the story for the first time and those who remember many of its lines fondly as they read the story aloud for their young listeners.
